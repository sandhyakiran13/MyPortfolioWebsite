Year Two of Fellowship

Elia Sarwat School

Classroom Culture

Print-Rich and Interactive Walls

Visual aids and interactive displays reinforced learning and encouraged daily recall of concepts.

Star Attendance

Monthly rewards for full attendance motivated students to come to school regularly, promoting responsibility and consistency.

Group of the Week

Students worked in heterogeneous groups, with behavior and academic progress tracked through a points system. The highest-scoring group earned rewards, encouraging teamwork, collaboration, and self-management.

Buddy System

Students with higher reading levels supported peers with lower levels, fostering empathy, peer learning, and communication.

Classroom Jobs

Students took on roles like line leader, resource manager, and library monitor. This system instilled ownership, accountability, and leadership skills.

Energizers and Brain Breakers

Daily activities like breathing exercises and student-led energizers (e.g., Talha leading meditation or Sheeba leading icebreakers) helped students refocus and develop mindfulness.

Community and Home Visits

Visiting students' homes helped me build trust, understand their needs better, and strengthen relationships with families.

Academics

Activity-Based Learning

Students engaged in collaborative activities and projects, enhancing creativity and deepening understanding.

Guided Lessons

Carefully planned lessons improved comprehension and built strong conceptual foundations.

Story Annotation

Students practiced analyzing and annotating stories, developing critical thinking skills.

Extra Support

I conducted extra classes for struggling students to provide targeted academic help.

DEAR (Drop Everything and Read)

Dedicated reading time improved students' reading habits. For example, one student, Saiba, completed Matilda in a month, showcasing her growing interest in reading.

Access and Exposure

Social and Emotional Learning (SEL)

Morning SEL sessions helped students explore values like equality, justice, and empathy. Resources from Project Bhawna and India & I supported these sessions.

Art Education

With the support of Saturday Art Class, students attended weekly art lessons, fostering creativity and emotional well-being.

Theater Experience

I took my students to NCPA to watch a live performance based on Panchatantra stories. The experience captivated them and reinforced lessons about honesty and other values in a memorable way.

The Right Pitch (TRP)

I revived this sports program for 25 boys and 14 girls from Grades 3 to 7. Despite initial challenges like parental hesitation, I engaged families and formed boys’ and girls’ teams. Our Junior Girls' team won 3rd place in the TRP tournament, demonstrating leadership, sportsmanship, and resilience.

Balanand Program

Collaborating with the Prafulla Art Foundation, I enrolled 30 students in an eight-lesson art program. Students explored their creativity, collaborated with peers, and received certificates upon completion.
