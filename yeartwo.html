Year Two of Fellowship

Elia Sarwat English High School
The Rising Stars

In my second year of fellowship, I had the privilege of teaching 31 Grade 3 students at Elia Sarwat English High School in Malvani, one of Mumbai’s largest slum areas. As the first Teach For India (TFI) fellow in the school, I worked with students from underprivileged backgrounds, most of whom were first- or second-generation learners.

Reflecting on the challenges faced by my Grade 8 students in reading and speaking English last year, I focused on building strong foundations in literacy and numeracy for my Grade 3 class. My vision was to create a classroom where students developed essential skills like critical thinking, collaboration, creativity, and communication. My goal was to help them lead themselves and others in the future.

Access and Exposure

Social and Emotional Learning (SEL)

Morning SEL sessions helped students explore values like equality, justice, and empathy. Resources from Project Bhawna and India & I supported these sessions.

Art Education

With the support of Saturday Art Class, students attended weekly art lessons, fostering creativity and emotional well-being.

Theater Experience

I took my students to NCPA to watch a live performance based on Panchatantra stories. The experience captivated them and reinforced lessons about honesty and other values in a memorable way.

The Right Pitch (TRP)

I revived this sports program for 25 boys and 14 girls from Grades 3 to 7. Despite initial challenges like parental hesitation, I engaged families and formed boys’ and girls’ teams. Our Junior Girls' team won 3rd place in the TRP tournament, demonstrating leadership, sportsmanship, and resilience.

Balanand Program

Collaborating with the Prafulla Art Foundation, I enrolled 30 students in an eight-lesson art program. Students explored their creativity, collaborated with peers, and received certificates upon completion.

Elia Utsav

Elia Utsav, the school’s exhibition and competition, this year focused on the theme of Indian states. Grade 3, along with Grade 6, represented the culture of Jammu and Kashmir. My students confidently showcased their learning through a Kashmiri dance performance, presentations on geography and history, and creative displays. After a month of preparation, Grades 3 and 6 proudly secured 3rd place in the competition, making it a memorable experience for everyone involved.
